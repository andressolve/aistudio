Mission 01 — The Magic Word
01
Image Generation
One Word Changes Everything
10 min
›

Here's something wild: you can change the entire feeling of an image by adding just one word to your prompt. Let's prove it.

Your Mission
Start with this exact prompt. Generate it.

"A dragon sitting on top of a mountain"

Now add ONE word to change the feeling. Generate it again.
Try each of these, one at a time:

→ "A baby dragon sitting on top of a mountain"
→ "A terrifying dragon sitting on top of a mountain"
→ "A sleeping dragon sitting on top of a mountain"

Look at all four images. One word. Totally different picture.
💡
Why this matters: Every word you type is an instruction. The AI listens to ALL of them. So picking the right words is like having a superpower — one word can completely change what you get.
// what happens with each word
No extra word

"A dragon sitting on top of a mountain"

You'll probably get a big, generic-looking dragon. Fine, but nothing special.

Add "baby"

"A baby dragon sitting on top of a mountain"

Now it's small and cute. Totally different feeling — you want to protect it instead of run from it.

Add "terrifying"

"A terrifying dragon sitting on top of a mountain"

Dark, scary, probably bigger teeth and glowing eyes. One word made it a villain.

After you try these three, pick your own word and add it. What happens?

  1. Generate the basic dragon prompt. Look at it.
  2. Add "baby" — generate again. What changed?
  3. Add "terrifying" — generate again. What changed?
  4. Add "sleeping" — generate again. What changed?
  5. Now pick your OWN word. What do you want the dragon to feel like?

Mission 02 — Style Swap
02
Image Generation
Same Picture, Different Universe
10 min
›

You just learned that one word changes the feeling. Now here's another trick: you can make the AI draw the same thing in completely different art styles.

Your Mission
Pick a character you like. It can be from a book, a game, or one you make up.

Write a short sentence about them. Example:
"A knight standing in the rain holding a broken shield"

Now generate it three times. Each time, add a different style at the end:

Version 1 — add "in the style of a manga panel"
Version 2 — add "realistic photograph, moody lighting"
Version 3 — add any style YOU want (see list below)
🎨
The cool part: The knight is the same. The rain is the same. The broken shield is the same. But the style makes it feel like a totally different story. Manga feels like an action scene. Realistic feels sad and heavy. You get to choose what feeling the viewer gets.
// example — a knight in the rain
Version 1 — Manga

Prompt:
"A knight standing in the rain holding a broken shield, in the style of a manga panel, bold ink lines, dramatic"

Why a director would pick this style: It feels like the moment before the hero gets back up and wins.

Version 2 — Realistic

Prompt:
"A knight standing in the rain holding a broken shield, realistic photograph, moody lighting, dark sky"

Why a director would pick this style: It feels real and painful — like this person actually fought and lost.

Version 3 — Pixel Art

Prompt:
"A knight standing in the rain holding a broken shield, pixel art, 16-bit retro video game style"

Why a director would pick this style: It feels like a classic video game — the same scene but now it's an adventure, not a tragedy.

Styles to try

manga · pixel art · oil painting · watercolor · claymation · stained glass · retro movie poster · pencil sketch · Studio Ghibli · dark fantasy concept art · comic book cover

  1. Pick your character. Write one sentence about them.
  2. Generate Version 1 — manga style.
  3. Generate Version 2 — realistic photo style.
  4. Generate Version 3 — any style you want.
  5. Which one is your favourite? Tell someone why.

Mission 03 — Fix It
03
AI Video
Make It, Watch It, Fix It
10 min
›

When you make a video with AI, the first version is almost never perfect. That's normal — even professional filmmakers reshoot scenes many times. Today you're going to make a video clip, find what's wrong, and fix it with better words.

Your Mission
Pick one of these scenes (or make up your own):

→ A wolf howling on a cliff at night
→ A spaceship flying through a storm
→ A samurai drawing his sword slowly

Ask Claude to help you write a prompt for Kling.
Generate it. Watch it.

Now: what's ONE thing that doesn't look right?
Tell Claude what's wrong. Ask it to fix just that part.
Generate Version 2.
🎥
The trick: Don't rewrite the whole thing. If the wolf looks good but the sky is wrong, only change the sky words. If the motion is weird, only change the action words. Fix one thing at a time.
// example — a wolf howling
Version 1

Prompt:
"A wolf standing on a cliff howling at the moon. Night sky with stars. Camera slowly zooms in. Dark and dramatic."

You watch it. What's wrong?
The wolf doesn't actually howl — it just stands there with its mouth closed.

Version 2 — Fix the action

What you tell Claude:
"The wolf isn't howling. Can you change the prompt so the wolf has its head tilted up and its mouth wide open, howling loud?"

New prompt:
"A wolf on a cliff tilts its head back and howls with its mouth wide open toward the full moon. Night sky with stars. Camera slowly zooms in. Dark and dramatic."

Version 2 looks way better. The wolf is actually doing something now.

That's the whole process: make it → watch it → say what's wrong → fix those words → try again.

  1. Pick your scene.
  2. Ask Claude to write you a Kling prompt for it.
  3. Generate Version 1 on Kling. Watch it.
  4. What's wrong? Tell Claude the problem.
  5. Generate Version 2. Is it better?

Mission 04 — Tell a Story
04
AI Video
Two Shots, One Story
10 min
›

One video clip is just a clip. But two clips that go together tell a story. This is how real movies work — each shot connects to the next one.

Your Mission
Make two video clips that go together. Think of it as BEFORE and AFTER:

Shot 1 — Something calm or normal
Shot 2 — Something changes

When someone watches Shot 1 then Shot 2 back to back, they should understand what happened.
🧠
The trick to making two shots feel connected: Keep something the same in both. Same character. Same place. Same weather. If everything changes, it feels like two random clips. If the place stays the same but the mood changes, it feels like a story.
// example ideas — before and after
Idea A — The Storm

Shot 1: "A small fishing boat floating on a calm, flat ocean. Sunny sky. Gentle waves. Peaceful and quiet. Cinematic."

Shot 2: "The same small fishing boat rocking wildly in a massive storm. Dark clouds, huge waves crashing, rain pouring down. Dramatic and intense. Cinematic."

Idea B — The Discovery

Shot 1: "A kid walking through a dark cave with a small torch. Shadows on the walls. Camera follows from behind. Quiet and mysterious."

Shot 2: "The same kid steps into a huge cavern filled with glowing crystals. Bright blue and purple light everywhere. The kid stops and looks up in amazement. Camera pulls back wide to show the scale."

Idea C — The Standoff

Shot 1: "A lone cowboy walks slowly down an empty dusty street. Wind blows. Camera wide. Sunset light. Tense and quiet."

Shot 2: "Close-up of the cowboy's hand reaching slowly for the gun on his hip. His eyes narrow. Camera zooms in tight. Intense."

Notice: each pair keeps the same character and place. Only the mood changes between shots. That's what makes it feel like a story.

  1. Pick a BEFORE and AFTER idea (use one above or make your own).
  2. Ask Claude to write the two Kling prompts.
  3. Generate Shot 1. Watch it.
  4. Generate Shot 2. Watch it.
  5. Watch them back to back. Does it feel like a story?
